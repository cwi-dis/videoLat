Getting Started

These are concise instructions, more complete information is available on the videoLat.org website.

Before you can do real measurements you must obtain calibrations for your input and output devices. Try downloading calibrations from videolat.org first. Calibrations are shown for your specific iPhone or iPad type. If the calibrations you need are not available you must do them yourself.

Camera Calibration using Remote Calibrated Screen

For this you need a second device (iPhone, iPad, Mac) with videoLat for which you already have calibrations. Both devices should be on the same Wi-Fi network.

On this device select New Calibration, then Reception Calibrate using Other Device. Select the camera you want to calibrate. Point it so that you can see the screen of the other device.

On the other, calibrated, device select New Measurement, then QR Code Screen Helper. A QR-code will be shown on the transmitting device. When this QR-code is picked up by the camera of the device to calibrate it will connect to the transmitting device over Wi-Fi. Check the status indicators on the top of the device screen.

On the device to calibrate press Run, and after a few hundred measurements Stop. You will be shown the results of the measurement and if it looks good you can save the calibration.

Screen Calibration using Remote Calibrated Camera

For this you need a second device (iPhone, iPad, Mac) with videoLat for which you already have calibrations. Both devices should be on the same Wi-Fi network.

On the other, calibrated, device select New Measurement, then QR Code Camera Helper.

On this device select New Calibration, then Transmission Calibrate using Other Device. Point the camera of the calibrated device at the QR-code shown on the device to calibrate it will connect to the master device over Wi-Fi. Check the status indicators on the top of the device screen.

On the master device press Run, and after a few hundred measurements Stop. You will be shown the results of the measurement, on both devices.

Video measurement

For a one-way measurement you need two devices with videoLat and the required calibrations. The device that transmits QR codes over video must be on a network (Wi-Fi, Cellular or Ethernet) that is reachable by the receiver device. The transmitter device shows QR codes, which should be picked up by the camera of the system-under-test. The output screen of the system-under-test should be picked up by the receiver videoLat camera. It will transmit the QR-codes detected back to the transmitter videoLat over the network.

This measurement can be run with QR Code Transmission to Camera Helper and QR Code Camera Helper, but it can also be run using QR Code Reception from Screen Helper and QR Code Screen Helper. The results should be identical, and the only difference is which device has the user interface to control (start, stop) the measurement. With mobile phones or tablets it is probably most convenient to use QR Code Reception from Screen Helper because that screen (with the buttons) is probably easier to see.

Select the master (user interface) and helper measurements on the two devices. Point the camera of the system under test at the screen showing the QR code. Point the camera of the receiving videoLat at the screen of the system under test. The QR code should now be detected and the receiver videoLat will contact the sender videoLat over the network. A few initial QR codes will be exchanged to determine parameters such as network roundtrip time..

On the master device press Run, and after a few hundred measurements Stop. You will be shown the results of the measurement, on both devices.

Roundtrip calibration and measurement

Note that roundtrip measurements are in general not very easy on an iPhone or iPad because you cannot position the camera and screen independently.

Select New Calibration, then QR Code Roundtrip Calibration. Select the camera you want to use. Use a mirror (or 2 mirrors) to make sure that camera gets a good view of the whole screen. Press Continue. You will see QR-codes and the Count: field will go from 10 to 0. When you see the blue screen you press Run. After enough measurements (a few hundred) press Stop. You will be shown the results of the measurement.

See the instruction video on the videoLat.org website for instructions on how to run a round-trip video delay measurement.

Viewing measurements

Here you see the minimum, maximum and average of your measurement, plus information on where and how the measurement was done. You also see graphs with the individual measured delays and the distribution of those delays.

Using the Action button you can print or email your measurement in various forms. You can also delete your measurement, and for calibrations you can upload them to videolat.org so other people can download it.

Privacy Policy

VideoLat does not transmit any data to cloud services, except when you upload a calibration measurement. The full privacy policy can be viewed at https://videolat.org/privacypolicy.
