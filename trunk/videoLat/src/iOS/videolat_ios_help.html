Getting Started

These are concise instructions, more complete help is available on the videoLat.org website.

Before you can do real measurements you must obtain calibrations for your input and output devices. Try downloading calibrations from videolat.org first. If the calibrations you need are not available you must do them yourself.

Roundtrip calibration

Select New Calibration, then Video Roundtrip Calibration. Select the camera you want to use. Use a mirror (or 2 mirrors) to make sure that camera gets a good view of the whole screen. Press Continue. You will see QR-codes and the Count: field will go from 10 to 0. When you see the blue screen you press Run. After enough measurements (a few hundred) press Stop. You will be shown the results of the measurement.

Camera Calibration using Remote Calibrated Screen

For this you need a second device (iPhone, iPad, Mac) with videoLat for which you already have calibrations. Both devices should be on the same Wi-Fi network.

On this device select New Calibration, then Camera Calibration using Remote Calibrated Screen. Select the camera you want to calibrate. Point it so that you can see the screen of the other device.

On the other, calibrated, device select New Measurement, then Video Transmission (Master,Server). A QR-code will be shown on the master device and is picked up by the slave device, and it will connect to the master device over Wi-Fi, check the status indicators on the top of the slave device screen.

On the master device press Run, and after a few hundred measurements Stop. You will be shown the results of the measurement, on both devices.

Screen Calibration using Remote Calibrated Camera

For this you need a second device (iPhone, iPad, Mac) with videoLat for which you already have calibrations. Both devices should be on the same Wi-Fi network.

On the other, calibrated, device select New Measurement, then Video Reception (slave,Client). Make sure the camera of this slave device can see the screen of the master device (the device you are calibrating).

On the master device select New Calibration, then Screen Calibrate using Remote Calibrated Camera (Master,Server). A QR-code will be shown on the master device and is picked up by the slave device, and it will connect to the master device over Wi-Fi, check the status indicators on the top of the slave device screen.

On the master device press Run, and after a few hundred measurements Stop. You will be shown the results of the measurement, on both devices.

Roundtrip measurement

See the instruction video on the videoLat.org website for instructions on how to run a round-trip video delay measurement.

One-way measurement

For a one-way measurement you need two devices with videoLat and the required calibrations. The master device must be on a network (Wi-Fi or Cellular) that is reachable by the slave device.

On the slave device do New Measurement, then Video Reception (slave,Client). Make sure the camera of this slave device can see the screen of the system-under-test.

On the master device do New Measurement, then Video Transmission (Master,Server). Make sure the screen of the master device is picked up by the camera of the system-under-test. A QR-code will be shown on the master device and is picked up by the slave device, and it will connect to the master device over Wi-Fi, check the status indicators on the top of the slave device screen.

On the master device press Run, and after a few hundred measurements Stop. You will be shown the results of the measurement, on both devices.

Viewing measurements

Here you see the minimum, maximum and average of your measurement, plus information on where and how the measurement was done. You also see graphs with the individual measured delays and the distribution of those delays.

Using the Action button you can print or email your measurement in various forms. You can also delete your measurement, and for calibrations you can upload them to videolat.org so other people can download it.
